This Month’s Austin Highlight

Gina Helrich

PhD Founder & Principal Consultant, Third Coast InnovateHER Co-Founder, recruitHER Founder, Feminist Hack

“At present, only 26% of programmers in the U.S. are women, and just 1 in 14 tech employees in Silicon Valley is black or Latino. Moving the needle on diversity is critical for the tech industry and promises to have an outsize impact on society at large. We need to work together to build a tech workforce that accurately reflects the diversity of the millions of individuals whose lives are shaped by its products.

For workers, the tech sector often offers higher salaries than other industries, boasts one of the smallest gender pay gaps compared to other professions, and offers a unique opportunity to play a role in shaping our collective future. For companies, a diverse workplace is proven to drive better results, increase innovation, and more accurately reflect a company’s user base, resulting in better decisions. Research has repeatedly shown that companies with more diverse and inclusive teams have lower turnover, higher customer satisfaction, higher productivity, and higher profitability. Diversity is a win-win for tech professionals and the companies who employ them.”
